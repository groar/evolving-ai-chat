## Normalized Summary
The Settings panel (and the experience more broadly) feels assembled incrementally rather than designed holistically. The user wants a clean-slate UX design pass on Settings — and other surfaces — to make the app feel coherent and intentional.

## PM Notes
- Settings currently has: Connections, Works offline, Updates (channel toggle), Early Access (details block), Notices, Changelog — all stacked in a narrow sheet.
- The panel is overloaded. Works offline is static info that doesn't belong in Settings. Changelog is a history panel. These mixed concerns reduce clarity.
- "Other parts of the experience" is deliberately broad — implementers should focus Settings for this ticket, noting where other surfaces (top bar, composer area, conversations sidebar) could benefit from a follow-up design pass.
- This feedback is tightly coupled with F-20260304-004 (design guidelines) — the settings rethink should be informed by the guidelines doc once that exists.

## Triage Decision
- Decision: create T-0068 (Settings UX holistic rethink), sequenced after T-0066 (design guidelines)
- Rationale: Settings is the highest-friction non-chat surface. A guided redesign within the new design system will pay dividends for subsequent surface improvements.
