## Raw Feedback (Sanitized)
"We should find another way to present the changelog. It is impossible to use as it is, there are too many cards, in a crowded, narrow space, and anyway it does not seem convenient. We should find more creative ways to present this in a functional way."

## Normalized Summary
The changelog section inside the Settings sheet accumulates an unbounded list of dense cards in a 400 px-wide panel, making it unusable once several changes exist. A different presentation model is needed — one that scales and feels intentional.

## PM Notes
- The changelog currently lives at the bottom of the Settings sheet, which is a config surface — a mismatch of mental models (history vs. settings).
- The narrow width (min(400px, 90vw)) forces cards to wrap text and truncate aggressively.
- As the number of patches grows (M8 delivers an autonomous loop), the problem will compound.
- Related to F-20260303-002 (T-0065: settings crowding), but this is specifically about the changelog's IA and scale problem, not just spacing.

## Triage Decision
- Decision: create T-0067 (Changelog UX redesign — dedicated Activity sheet)
- Rationale: Changelog and Settings address different jobs-to-be-done (history vs. config). Splitting them into separate surfaces solves both the space and mental-model problems. The existing Sheet pattern (already used for Conversations and Improvement) is a natural extension.
- Revisit Trigger: after T-0067 is accepted
